Profit and Loss Question 1

Question): A merchant bought a shirt for $40 and sold it for $60. What is the profit?

Answer: The profit is $20.

Explanation: The profit is calculated as the selling price ($60) minus the cost price ($40), which equals $20.

Profit and Loss Question 2

Question):If a bookshop bought a book for $15 and sold it for $20, what is the profit percentage?

Answere : The profit percentage is 33.33%.

Explanation : Profit percentage is calculated as (Profit / Cost Price) * 100. In this case, it's (5 / 15) * 100 = 33.33%.

Profit and Loss Question 3

Question):A company bought 1000 shares at $10 each and sold them at $12 each. Calculate the profit.

Answer: The profit is $2000.

Explanation: The profit is calculated as (Selling Price - Cost Price) * Number of Shares = ($12 - $10) * 1000 = $2000.
