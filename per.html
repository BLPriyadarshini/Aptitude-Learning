Percentage Question 1

Question: If a product originally costs $50 and is now on sale for 20% off, what is the sale price?

Answer: The sale price is $40.

Explanation: The sale price is calculated as (Original Price) - (Original Price * Percentage Discount). In this case, it's $50 - ($50 * 0.20) = $40.

Percentage Question 2

Question: If a student scored 80% on a test with a maximum score of 100, what is the student's actual score?

Answer: The student's actual score is 80.

Explanation: The actual score is calculated as (Percentage Scored) * (Maximum Score). In this case, it's 0.80 * 100 = 80.

Percentage Question 3

Question: If a store marks up the cost of an item by 30% and sells it for $65, what is the original cost of the item?

Answer: The original cost is $50.

Explanation: The original cost is calculated as (Selling Price) / (1 + Percentage Markup). In this case, it's $65 / (1 + 0.30) = $50.

Percentage Question 5

Question: A product's price was reduced by 25% and is now $75. What was its original price?

Answer: The original price was $100.

Explanation: The original price is calculated as (Sale Price) / (1 - Percentage Reduction). In this case, it's $75 / (1 - 0.25) = $100.

Percentage Question 6

Question: If a company's profits increased by 50% from last year's $200,000, what is the current year's profit?

Answer: The current year's profit is $300,000.

Explanation: The current year's profit is calculated as (Previous Year's Profit) * (1 + Percentage Increase). In this case, it's $200,000 * (1 + 0.50) = $300,000.

Percentage Question 13

Question: If a company offers a 15% bonus on sales commissions, and an employee's commission is $1,500, what is the total commission with the bonus?

Answer: The total commission with the bonus is $1,725.

Explanation: The total commission with the bonus is calculated as (Commission) + (Commission * Percentage Bonus). In this case, it's $1,500 + ($1,500 * 0.15) = $1,725.
